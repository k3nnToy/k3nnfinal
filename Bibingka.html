Bibingka

Bibingka is a beloved Filipino rice cake made with glutinous rice and coconut milk, traditionally cooked in clay pots lined with banana leaves. It’s often topped with salted eggs, cheese, and butter, offering a deliciously smoky flavor. Popular during Christmas season and Simbang Gabi!

Its soft, fluffy texture combined with its sweet and salty taste makes Bibingka a festive treat that captures the heart of Filipino celebrations.

Cultural Significance

Bibingka is a beloved Filipino rice cake traditionally eaten during the Christmas season, especially after Simbang Gabi, the nine early morning masses before Christmas Day. It is usually sold by vendors outside churches, served hot on banana leaves, and often enjoyed with family and friends. The way it is cooked—using clay pots and coals—highlights Filipino ingenuity and culinary heritage. More than just a holiday treat, bibingka represents Filipino faith, generosity, and the spirit of community, making it a meaningful part of the country's festive celebrations.
