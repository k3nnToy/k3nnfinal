Puto Bumbong

Puto Bumbong is a traditional Filipino rice delicacy made from a special variety of purple glutinous rice (pirurutong). The rice is soaked, ground, and then steamed in bamboo tubes (bumbong) using a unique steaming device called a lansungan. Once cooked, the rice cake is carefully removed from the tubes and served hot on banana leaves, topped with grated coconut, muscovado or brown sugar, and sometimes melted butter or margarine.

Its purple color, chewy texture, and aromatic toppings make it one of the most beloved native treats during the holiday season.

Cultural Significance

Puto Bumbong is strongly associated with the Christmas season, especially after Simbang Gabi, a nine-day series of early morning masses. It is commonly sold outside churches and enjoyed hot by families and churchgoers.
