Biko

Biko is a traditional Filipino delicacy made from glutinous rice (malagkit), coconut milk, and brown sugar, topped with latik (caramelized coconut cream curds). It has a rich, sticky texture and a sweet, nutty flavor from the coconut and sugar combination. Biko is typically brown in color due to the caramelized sugar and is served in square or rectangular slices..

Cultural Significance

Biko is commonly prepared for special occasions, such as fiestas, holidays, birthdays, and family gatherings. It represents Filipino hospitality and celebration, as it is usually shared with guests and neighbors. The use of native ingredients and the traditional method of slow cooking over fire highlight Filipino patience, resourcefulness, and love for home-cooked food. Biko is a symbol of warmth, togetherness, and Filipino culinary heritage.
