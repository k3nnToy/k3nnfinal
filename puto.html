Puto

PutoTypically shaped into small, round portions, puto can be topped with cheese, salted egg slices, or even flavored with pandan or ube (purple yam) for extra color and taste. It's commonly cooked in specially designed molds and steamed until light and fluffy.

Puto symbolizes community and celebration in Filipino culture, often appearing at fiestas, family gatherings, and religious occasions. Its simplicity, versatility, and comforting taste have made it a timeless favorite across generations.

Cultural Significance

Puto is one of the most well-known and widely enjoyed native delicacies in the Philippines. It is served during birthdays, fiestas, holidays, and even funerals, often paired with savory dishes like dinuguan (pork blood stew). Its presence at gatherings reflects Filipino values of hospitality, sharing, and celebration. Because of its simplicity and versatility, puto has remained a staple in Filipino cuisine across generations.
